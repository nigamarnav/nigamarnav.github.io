BookBuddy - AR Chatbot


Academic Project (June - August 2020)

Theme:
Exploring the possibilities of Immersive Technology in Education.

Team:
Arnav (Myself) - User Research, Design and Development
Prashant - 3D Modelling and Development

Problem Statement:
How might we address the challenges of remote learning for children and enhance their learning experience?

Design Process

Problem Space Exploration - Initial Research


There are three main stakeholders who are directly connected to any learning system - students, teachers and parents. We went through many articles, blogs and post on internet to understand concerns with each stakeholder. We also attended a webinar by 'OpenIDEO' on Remote Learning where Parents, Teachers and Researchers were sharing their thoughts. We found that distance learning problems with parents were directly dependent on students' problems.


Problems with Students


Problems with Teachers

Primary Research


We attended some online classes to closely observe children's activities and potential problem areas.
The screenshots taken during these classes show how some children were focused, some were distracted and some were using the camera as a mirror.



After talking to their teachers, we realized lack of pedagogical training for online-teaching and digital literacy were major issues among teachers. In first view, it was something that can not be solved by simply providing a digital solution. Therefore we decided to move forward with inclination towards students' problems.


Experience Map of students in Online Class and their Motivation at Day 1 and Day 30.


Most of the time students and teachers were rushing to complete the syllabus and class-activities were limited to answering chapter-questions and completing the assignments. This was extremely disinteresting for subjects like Social Studies, Environmental Science, Ethics etc.


User-Persona

Market Research


There were many resources available from where students were taking help. We made a list of Learning Resources according to their teaching-method. YouTube was one of the major contributor in remote rearning (Could be considered as video-based learning).


Current Learning Resources

While conduting research on Learning Resources, we found that some textbooks were providing QR codes to help students. This was a beautiful way to introduce marker-based AR.

Applause:
In December 2020, Ranjit Disale, the man who brought the idea of ​​QR codes in textbooks, received the Global Teachers Award 2020. Kudos!!
