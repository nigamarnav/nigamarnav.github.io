Worry Not


D'source Corona Design Challenge (April - May 2020)

About the Chellenge:
D'source Corona Design Challenge was a Design Competition organised by IDC IIT Bombay in response to global crisis of COVID-19.

Brief:
'Worry Not' was Winning entry in open category. It is a mobile application that can be used by mental health patients during isolation to take advice from the Psychiatrists based on their mental health-condition.

Understanding the Problem Domain

To understand the problem, I went through news-articles, reports, and surveys done by various organization. Some of my key findings were:


  • PTSD has been found in many cases for longer duration of quarantine.
  • Fear of infection and stigmatization of pandemic makes it worse.
  • Boredom and bad communication lead to frustration.
  • People are seeking many types of information from many different sources.

Problem Statement

How might we can help people who are going through bad mental state in this unseen situation of COVID-19?

Desk Research

In this project, Psychology was driving my research. To grasp a deeper understanding of the problem I went through the book 'Psychiatry of Pandemics' along with going through Reports and Analysis by Mental Health Practioners and Researchers. Based on the desk research I divided problem in four subparts.

  • Vulnerable population : People, who are at the edge of mental health conditions in the time of crisis.
  • Changes : What major changes have occurred due to COVID-19 pandemic.
  • Mental Health Triggers : Common triggers that are behind worse conditions.
  • Cultural and Social Aspect : How people handle the situation, cultural and social values defines a major role here.

Primary Research

It was difficult to find those who were facing the situation. Also, it was a sensitive issue so I was a little careful about it. I found a Facebook group of Coranavirus Patients where they were sharing their stories to uplift themselves and others. I went through the groups' posts, comments and chatted with some of them. Read Medium Article for detailed analysis.

Insights

  • Religious significance and various interpretations of religious texts is playing major role in stigmas. (Sudden increase of religious posts and messages in social media.)
  • Faith in doctors make people feel safe and secure. (Reported by patients and doctors.)

Meeting the need of regular health monitoring.

In case of emergency, send a message describing the condition or contact doctor to get help.

Doctor's Prescription

Doctors can write personal advice on Patient's wall. (In backend, this will work like broadcast to patients with similar situation.)
